Airbnb Stories

Telling stories is a powerful, personal way to communicate. That's why Airbnb collects stories from its hosts and puts them [on its web site](https://www.airbnb.ca/stories) and uses them liberally in its publicity. When the economic crash hit, [Tama](https://www.airbnb.ca/stories/new-york/one-way-forward)’s livelihood as a painter and real estate broker was threatened. A serious health condition was only increasing her expenses, with prescription costs at times topping $1000 per month. Receiving guests not only introduced her to new friends—it allowed her to eat, pay her bills, and stay in her home.
