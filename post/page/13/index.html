"Real-Time Entertainment" outpacing the web

As measured by the byte, the Internet is increasingly a video-consumption medium dominated by a small number of large providers. Wired reports that “In the evenings, Netflix accounts for more than 40 percent of U.S. bandwidth usage, by some measurements”, more than the cumulative amount of web browsing traffic. So far as traffic is concerned, the Internet is becoming (as Tim Wu has warned us it might) a medium for commercial broadcast transmission of studio-produced products. Netflix’s success is starting to sideline peer-to-peer traffic in movies, just as iTunes sidelined peer-to-peer traffic in music.
